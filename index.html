What was life like in Ancient Egypt?

Diving into it, and why it was so important to humanity

Climate

Daily life in Ancient Egypt revolved around a giant river called the Nile and the fertile land it produced along its banks. The flooding of the Nile refined the soil and in return gave good annual harvests to the Egyptians. However, Ancient Egypt's climate resembles today's; a dry, hot desert climate with very minimal to no rainfall. Egypt's coastal zone was blessed with winds coming from the Mediterranean Sea, while temperatures inland were scorching, even more so during summer season.
